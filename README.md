# Digital Forensic Investigation of Cyberbullying through Deepfake Images and Social Media Abuse

## Overview
This project explores the forensic investigation of cyberbullying incidents involving deepfake images and social media abuse. It focuses on identifying, analyzing, and documenting digital manipulation techniques used in synthetic media and online harassment scenarios.

The aim is to understand how digital forensic methods can be applied to detect altered media, preserve evidence, and support cybercrime investigations in social media environments.

---

## Objectives
- Investigate cyberbullying cases involving deepfake images and manipulated media.
- Apply digital forensic principles to identify digital artifacts and evidence of manipulation.
- Understand how social media platforms can be used in cyber harassment cases.
- Develop structured forensic reporting methods for analysis and documentation.

---

## Methodology
The investigation followed a structured digital forensic approach:

1. **Identification**
   - Defined the scope of cyberbullying involving synthetic media.
   - Identified potential sources of digital evidence from social media environments.

2. **Preservation**
   - Considered methods for preserving digital evidence integrity.
   - Maintained chain-of-custody principles in theoretical analysis.

3. **Analysis**
   - Examined potential deepfake indicators such as facial inconsistencies, lighting mismatches, and unnatural artifacts.
   - Reviewed social media content for signs of harassment patterns and manipulated media distribution.

4. **Documentation**
   - Recorded findings in a structured forensic reporting format.
   - Highlighted evidence interpretation and reasoning.
